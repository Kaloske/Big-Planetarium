Venus

Named after the Roman Goddess of Beauty, Venus has a thick atmosphere, making its surface hotter than Mercury!

Venus is surprisingly similar to earth, being approximately 95% the size of Earth and having 82% of its mass. The two also have an atmosphere containing carbon dioxide and nitrogen.

Unfortunately, the nature of Venus' conditions make it inhospitable to life, with its high temperatures and sulfuric acid rain being significant barriers to even unmanned exploration.

Facts!

  • While second from the Sun, Venus is hotter than Mercury, with temperatures reaching up to 475°C. This is because of its thick atmosphere.

  • Venus has a retrograde spin, meaning it rotates in the opposite direction (counter-clockwise). The sun rises in the west and sets in the east.

  • Venus takes 243 Earth Days to rotate once on its axis, but only 225 Earth Days to orbit the Sun. This makes one day on Venus longer than one year.
