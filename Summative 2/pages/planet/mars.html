Mars

The Red planet's distinct color reminded ancient cultures of blood and fire. For this reason, the Romans named the planet after Mars, the Roman God of War.

Mars has been a target of numerous unmanned rover and probe missions. No doubt influenced by the planet having relatively close day-night cycle, resources like water ice, and evidence of past water.

Pictures

Facts!

  • Its red and orange color comes from the presence of iron oxide.

  • Mars is roughly half the diameter of Earth

  • Olympus Mons on Mars is the tallest mountain in the solar system. For reference, it is three times taller than Mount Everest. A fitting fact for a mountain bearing the name of the home of the Greek Gods.
