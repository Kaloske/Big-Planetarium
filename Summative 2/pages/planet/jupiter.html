Jupiter

Jupiter is named after the King of the Roman Gods. The Gas Giant is the largest planet in our solar system and is composed of primarily hydrogen and helium.

To put its size into perspective, it has a diameter of 11 times that of Earth, and has a mass of around 2.5 times that of all planets in the solar system combined.

Facts!

  • Like all gas giants, Jupiter has no solid surface. However, as the pressure increases towards its center, the gasses get compressed into a liquid form.

  • Despite its size, one day on Jupiter takes only 9.9 Earth Hours.

  • The Great Red Spot is a storm that has been occuring for centuries. It is big enough fit Earth, and has wind speeds of 400-600 km/h.
