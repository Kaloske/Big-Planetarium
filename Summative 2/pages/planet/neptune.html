Neptune

The deep blue planet is named after Neptune, the Roman god of the sea. The planet was found via mathematical prediction in 1846 by Johann Galle.

Similar to Jupiter's "Great Red Spot", Neptune has its "Great Dark Spot", an Earth-sized anti-cyclone storm.

Facts!

  • Neptune's winds can reach over 2000km/h, making them the fastest winds in the solar system.

  • Neptune takes 164.8 Earth years to orbin the Sun. It has only one orbit since its discovery in 1846.

  • It is the furthest known planet orbiting the Sun, located over 4.5 billion km away.
